Peer Review Policy

Peer Review Process

All manuscripts submitted to JAES will follow a rigorous double-blind peer review process, where reviewers and authors remain anonymous throughout the process and the peer review comments will not be published.

  • New manuscripts will first be reviewed and assured for non- plagiarism by the managing editor. Manuscripts that do not conform to the journalâ€™s ethical policies and standards will be rejected directly. Manuscripts that passed the checking will proceed to the next stage.
  • The manuscript will be assigned to associate editors based on the expertise in the relevant field.
  • The associate editors will assign experts in the relevant field to review the manuscript. Reviewers should not work at the same institution as any of the authors, close collaborators or joint grant holders.
  • The reviewers are also required to evaluate the supplementary material if submitted.
  • The Editor-in-Chief / Deputy editor in chief or the academic editor designated by the Editor-in-Chief will make a final decision based on the comments(at least 2 per manuscript) of the reviewers:

Accepted, Accepted with minor correction, Revisions Required, Resubmit for Review or Rejected

Accepted

The manuscripts will be further processed for publication after intimating the same with authors by sending an acceptance letter.

Accepted with minor correction

Manuscripts need to be resubmitted after properly addressing the comments by the reviewer. The revised manuscript should be re-submitted within 7 days.

Revisions Required

The author should revise and resubmit the manuscript within 14-21 days.

Resubmit for Review

The author will have more time to revise and resubmit for the second round of review until the manuscript is accepted.

Rejected

The editor will directly inform the final decision to the authors and archive submission.

Any appeal regarding the review process and editorial decision can be intimated Pixous publication by letter to editor@pixouspublication.com and attach the detailed reasons for the appeal and the point-by-point reply to the reviewersâ€™ comments. The decision of appeal will be given by the Editor-in-Chief or editorial board of the journal according to the relevant information of the article. Decisions on appeals are final without exception.

The peer review process will be deemed to be completed for all accepted manuscripts. The manuscripts need to be copyedited, layout edited and proofread Before being published.

Publication Guidelines for Reviewer

Peer reviews are conducted as a double-blind process through the Pixous publication online submission system. As an editorâ€™s and/or reviewerâ€™s comments will determine the acceptance or rejection of manuscripts, these comments play an important role in the peer-review process.

  • Reviews should be conducted in a fair and objective manner. Remarks and suggestions should not contain insults or criticism directed at the author as opposed to the manuscript.
  • The author has the right to all the information published in his manuscript. Reviewers are not allowed to divulge or use that information for personal gain.
  • Uphold utmost confidentiality through the peer-review process.
  • If the reviewer feels that a manuscriptâ€™s subject does not fit within the scope of the journal, they may intimate the editorial office to take further action.
  • Possible areas of focus while conducting the review of manuscripts:
    • Potential ethical concerns
    • Technical errors
    • Logical errors (fallacies, lapses in logic, etc.)
    • Language errors that mar the clarity of the text
    • References :
      • Potential ethical concerns
      • Technical errors
      • Logical errors (fallacies, lapses in logic, etc.)

Publication Ethics for Publishers

  • To create and update plagiarism policies to stop unethical work submission.
  • To not discriminate been authors and evaluate work purely on the basis of its subject.
  • To handle conflicts of interest wisely and with discretion.
